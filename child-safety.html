Child Safety Standards

Last updated: April 19, 2026

Rizzmatch AI is a dating application strictly intended for adults aged 18 and above. The safety of minors is of the utmost importance to us. We have a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) in any form.

1. Age Requirement

Rizzmatch AI requires all users to be at least 18 years old. Users confirm their age during registration. Accounts found to belong to users under 18 are immediately and permanently terminated.

2. Zero Tolerance for CSAE

We have a strict zero-tolerance policy against child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any content that sexually exploits or endangers minors. This includes:

  • Any imagery, video, or text that sexually depicts or exploits minors
  • Grooming behaviour targeting minors
  • Any attempt to use the platform to contact or exploit minors

Any user found to be engaging in such behaviour will be immediately banned and reported to the relevant authorities.

3. Reporting Mechanisms

Rizzmatch AI provides in-app tools for users to report concerning behaviour:

  • Report a profile: Users can report any profile directly from the app for inappropriate content, suspicious behaviour, or concerns about a user's age.
  • Block a user: Users can immediately block any user they feel uncomfortable with.
  • Email reporting: Users can report child safety concerns directly to support.rizzmatch@gmail.com.

4. Response to Reports

All reports are reviewed promptly. When we identify CSAM or CSAE-related content or behaviour:

  • The account is immediately suspended pending investigation
  • Content is removed from the platform
  • We report to the National Centre for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) and relevant Indian authorities (CBI, Cyber Crime Portal) as required by law
  • We cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations

5. Compliance

Rizzmatch AI complies with all applicable Indian laws regarding child safety, including the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012, and the Information Technology Act, 2000. We report CSAM to the relevant national and regional authorities as required.

6. Contact

To report a child safety concern or CSAE-related content, contact our designated safety team at:
